Hammerknuden Loop


On the northern part of the island of Bornholm lies the spectacular nature area Hammerknuden. The rock formations on the island are breathtaking. The first part of the trail is through the city on the sidewalk. The rest is a lovely hilly trail along the coast with a glorious wealth of views, flowers and animals. As you move along the coast, you will also pass one out of two lighthouses at Hammerknuden.Discover this 6.8-km loop trail near Allinge, Bornholm. Generally considered a moderately challenging route.

Højlyngsstien Bornholm


Højlyngsstien is a trail which takes you across Bornholm. THe route passes Hammerknuden, Slotslyngen, Rutsker Højlyng, Rø Plantage, Almindingen, Ølene and Paradisbakkerne. You hike through different landscapes, like heath fields and large rocks. The trail is designed to give you the most spectacular nature experience of Bornholm. The trail largely runs on footpaths and gravel roads, there are some stretches on asphalt. There are lots of camping opportunities on the route, in shelters, in primitive campsites and in forest areas with free tenting. The trail is well marked.

South of the Aarhus city, in the Thors woods which are part of the larger Marselisborg-Moesgaard forest, lies the Deer Park. The area houses a wonderful population of sika and fallow deer which you can get really close-up to. The wild boars live in their own enclosure.

the Baltic Sea Cycle Route


This cycle route in the shape of a figure eight runs through some of the most beautiful areas of coastal Denmark, including the fjords west of the Small Belt bridge, the archipelago around South Fyn and the cliffs on the island of Møn. Island-hopping is the aim of the game in the southern section, where you will cycle over bridges or take your bike on cycle-friendly ferries to explore quiet islands and peaceful beaches, whilst continuing the loop round to the south of Jutland.

The Ancient Road


This historic hiking and cycling route leads from Padborg in South Jutland over moraine hills to Frederikshavn in the north of Jutland, with everything including cultural monuments, historical sights and amusement parks in-between. Once a route for pilgrims, drovers, merchants and craftsmen, today it gives you an opportunity to travel the backbone of Jutland from north to south and enjoy its varied nature and landscape.

The Border Route


This route traces the area where Northern Germany and Southern Jutland join (an area that celebrated a historic reunification in 2020). It runs from the North Sea to the Baltic Sea and takes a zigzag route across the border from Vidå lock near Højer to the top of the port of Flensburg.
