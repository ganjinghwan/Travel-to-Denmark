Land Transportation

Taxi and Car Rental

Taxi in Denmark are expensive compared to other transportation methods


Codan Taxi (+45-70-25-25-25) in Copenhagen offers service 24 hours


Taxi drivers in Denmark accept credit card payments


Most major companies are found at airports and train stations


Renting a car can be expensice, but the roads are well-maintained, with traffic congestion only a problem in a handful of large cities

Bike and Electric Scooter

Copenhagen's City Bike project, Bycyklen, is a network of electric bikes all over the city that you can rent from the street.


Each bike has a touchscreen tablet which can be used for navigation, payment and tourist info.


It is also easy to rent a bike from a local bike shop, pick up a Donkey rental bike on the street

Bus, Metro and Train

You can choose between different versions of the city pass which will give you unlimited access to buses, trains and the metro in a different zone ranging from 24 hours to 5 days


There is 5 Type of City Pass:


  • City Pass Small covers Copenhagen + the trip from the airport to the City of Copenhagen (zones 1-4)
  • City Pass Large covers Copenhagen + the Greater Copenhagen area (zones 1-99)
  • City Pass X-Large covers Copenhagen and all of Zealand + Lolland, Falster and Møn (zones 1-299)
  • City Pass West covers the Western part of Zealand (zones 101-199)
  • City Pass South covers the Southern part of Zealand + Lolland, Falster and Møn (zones 201-299)

You can buy your City Pass on the DOT Tickets app and in the ticket vending machines on all train and metro stations.

Harbour Bus

These boats are operated by the same carrier as normal buses.


Thus, City Pass can be used for the harbour bus too.


The Harbour Bus has 3 Ferry routes in Denmark with 12 Ferry stations.

Their Ferry routes cover an area from


  • Teglholmen (Teglholmens Østkaj) stop to the Refshaleøen (Refshalevej) stop
  • Orientkaj St. (Orientkaj) stop to the Teglholmen (Teglholmens Østkaj) stop

Air Transportation

Aviation

There are several daily flights from Copenhagen to cities around Denmark.


For example:

  • Norwegian fly directly to Aalborg, Billund and Karup
  • SAS have direct flights to Aarhus, Aalborg and Billund
  • Alsie Express fly between ønderborg and Copenhagen in just 35 minutes.
